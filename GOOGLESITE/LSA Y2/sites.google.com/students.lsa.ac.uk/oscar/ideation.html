Design Language

A successful website relies on a well-crafted design language. In the development of my website, I have opted for the following colour palette. I am confident that my selected colour scheme is lively and eye-catching.

The challenge with Google Sites lies in its limited customization options, making it difficult, sometimes impossible, to personalise to my needs. In order to incorporate distinct background colours, I hopped into Illustrator, creating solid colour backgrounds for each of my selected hues. These designs could then be uploaded for use on my website. 

I faced challenges when attempting to export a Google Slide to a satisfactory quality. I took time to troubleshoot and tried using an upscaler along with various converters. It became evident that Google Sites automatically downscales my content. Despite attempting to embed pictures using HTML, which proved unsuccessful, I experimented with eight different methods as shown to the right. 

Moving forward, I have decided to upload most of my work via slides.

For clarity, main reflections will be highlighted like below:

Problem: The default website looks bland.
Solution: Create a custom colour palette and coherent design language!
